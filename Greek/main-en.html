The course

Why learn Koine Greek?

The New Testament was written in Koine Greek, the common language of the Mediterranean world in the first century. Reading it in its original language allows you to access nuances, structures, and emphases that no translation can fully convey.

This course starts from zero: you will learn the alphabet, phonology, noun and verb morphology, and basic syntax of New Testament Greek — all explained clearly in English.

Methodology

How you will learn

I

Learn the system, not the exception

Greek grammar has a coherent internal logic. Each lesson explains patterns so you can recognize new forms without having seen them before.

II

Strategic vocabulary

The 300 most frequent words in the New Testament cover more than 80% of the text. This course prioritizes this high‑impact vocabulary from the very first lessons.

III

Reading from day one

Each lesson includes real New Testament verses to practice. It’s not grammar for grammar’s sake: everything is aimed at reading the Word in Greek.

IV

Cumulative progression

Each lesson builds on the previous one. By lesson 24 you will have covered 95% of the grammatical structures of the New Testament Greek.
