The course

Why learn Koine Greek?

The New Testament was written in Koine Greek, the common language of the Mediterranean world in the first century. Reading it in its original language gives you access to nuances, structures, and emphases that no translation can fully convey.

This course starts from zero: you will learn the alphabet, phonology, noun and verb morphology, and basic syntax of New Testament Greek — all explained clearly in English. Our method integrates three simultaneous approaches: grammar, vocabulary (1050 most frequent words), and progressive reading of the New Testament.

Methodology

How you will learn

I

Learn the system, not the exception

Greek grammar has a coherent internal logic. Each lesson explains patterns so you can recognize new forms without having seen them before.

II

Strategic vocabulary (1050 words)

The 1050 most frequent words of the New Testament cover more than 90% of the text. This course prioritizes this high‑impact vocabulary, introducing 15 words per lesson.

III

7 micro‑lessons per lesson

Each lesson is divided into 7 thematic micro‑lessons with drag‑and‑drop, flashcards, matching, and self‑assessment exercises to reinforce learning.

IV

Programmed reading

The programmed reading method breaks down content into small steps (micro‑lessons) that ensure steady progress and avoid cognitive overload.

V

Cumulative progression

Each lesson builds on the previous one. By lesson 70 you will have covered 95% of the grammatical structures of New Testament Greek.
