The course

Why learn Koine Greek?

The New Testament was written in Koine Greek. Reading it in its original language gives you access to nuances that no translation can fully convey. Our course uses the programmed reading method, which breaks learning down into small units (micro-lessons) so you can advance steadily and confidently.

This course starts from scratch: you will learn the alphabet, phonology, nominal and verbal morphology, and basic syntax of New Testament Greek — all explained clearly in Spanish. We integrate three simultaneous approaches: grammar, vocabulary (1400 most frequent words, covering 90% of the NT), and progressive reading of the New Testament.

Structured in 5 modules and 70 lessons, with a cumulative vocabulary of 1400 words (96% of the NT).

Methodology

How you will learn

I

Programmed text

Each lesson is divided into small units with immediate response, ensuring steady progress without overload.

II

Strategic vocabulary (1400 words)

The 1400 most frequent words in the New Testament cover more than 96% of the text. The course prioritizes this high-impact vocabulary, introducing 20 words per lesson.

III

Inductive reading

Learn grammar and vocabulary in their natural context by reading real NT passages from the early lessons.

IV

Spaced Repetition (SRS)

Integrated spaced repetition system to solidify vocabulary long-term with growing intervals.

V

Contextual analysis

Each lesson includes exercises for recognizing forms and analyzing NT clauses, preparing you for exegesis.
