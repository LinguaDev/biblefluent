The course

Why learn Biblical Hebrew?

The Old Testament was written in Biblical Hebrew. Reading it in its original language allows you to access nuances that no translation can fully convey. Our course uses the programmed reading method, breaking down learning into small, manageable steps so you can progress steadily and confidently.

This course starts from scratch: you will learn the alphabet, the vowel system (Masoretic points), nominal and verbal morphology (including the binyanim), and the basic syntax of Old Testament Hebrew — all clearly explained in Spanish. We integrate three simultaneous approaches: grammar, vocabulary (1,400 most frequent words, covering over 95% of the Tanakh), and progressive reading of the biblical text.

Structured in 5 modules and 70 lessons, with a cumulative vocabulary of 1,400 words (96% of the OT).

Methodology

How you will learn

I

Programmed text

Each lesson is divided into small units with immediate feedback, ensuring steady progress without overload.

II

Strategic vocabulary (1,400 words)

The 1,400 most frequent words of the Old Testament cover over 96% of the text. The course prioritizes this high-impact vocabulary, introducing 20 words per lesson.

III

Inductive reading

Learn grammar and vocabulary in their natural context, reading real passages from the Tanakh from the earliest lessons.

IV

Spaced Repetition (SRS)

An integrated spaced repetition system to solidify vocabulary long-term with increasing intervals.

V

Contextual analysis

Each lesson includes exercises in form recognition and clause analysis from the Tanakh, preparing you for exegesis.
